# Governance

Koru begins with a maintainer-led model under the BuilderKing GitHub organization. Governance should become more distributed as active, trusted contributors emerge.

## Decision model

- The build plan is the product and engineering source of truth during the planning stage.
- Small implementation decisions are made in focused pull requests.
- Changes to locked product behavior, privacy, security, storage, distribution, licensing, or public promises require an architecture or product decision record.
- Maintainers seek rough consensus but may make a documented decision when consensus cannot be reached.
- Security-sensitive releases require independent review from someone other than the author.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers review contributions, protect user trust, keep public claims accurate, publish releases, respond to vulnerabilities, enforce community standards, and disclose conflicts of interest.

## Becoming a maintainer

A contributor may be invited after sustained high-quality work, respectful collaboration, sound privacy and security judgment, and demonstrated follow-through. Access is least-privilege and may be scoped to documentation, website, application, or release operations.

## Repository protections

Before implementation begins, configure protected main, required reviews, required status checks, private vulnerability reporting, signed release tags, CODEOWNERS for sensitive paths, and separately protected release/deployment credentials.
